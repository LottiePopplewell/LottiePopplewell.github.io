daisy

Daisy Family Tree:
Daisies are not alone in their floral glory; they belong to a vast family that includes esteemed relatives like dahlias and sunflowers. With approximately 24,000 recognized species, the daisy family stands as one of the largest in the plant kingdom, boasting nearly 10 percent of the world's flowering plants.

Vibrant Gerberas:
Gerbera daisies enchant with their vibrant hues, available in a delightful spectrum of eight colors: yellow, white, pink, salmon, lavender, red, orange, and bicolor. These cheerful blooms add a splash of color to any garden or floral arrangement.

Diverse Daisy Species:
While the iconic image of a white flower with a yellow center often comes to mind, daisies encompass a diverse range of approximately 38 species. From traditional favorites to lesser-known varieties, daisies offer endless possibilities for gardeners and flower enthusiasts alike.

Shasta Daisy Care:
Shasta daisies, beloved for their classic charm, require periodic division every two to three years to maintain their health and vitality. By dividing clumps regularly, gardeners ensure these beloved blooms thrive and continue to grace gardens with their beauty.

The Story of Shasta Daisies:
Shasta daisies boast a fascinating lineage, bred from four distinct parent blooms to create the timeless backyard favorite cherished by gardeners around the world. Their rich history adds an extra layer of allure to these enduring symbols of natural beauty.

Intricate Floral Architecture:
Daisies captivate with their intricate floral structure, featuring a mesmerizing array of two types of florets. The symmetrical yellow florets at the center provide a striking contrast to the elongated petal structures within the white ring. This unique design serves as a welcoming landing pad for bees and butterflies, enhancing the daisy's ecological significance.

Global Presence:
Daisies bloom somewhere in the world every month of the year, showcasing their resilience and adaptability across diverse climates and landscapes. Whether gracing alpine meadows or sun-drenched fields, daisies symbolize nature's enduring beauty and vitality.

Symbolism of Innocence:
Daisies hold a cherished place in cultural symbolism, representing innocence and purity. During Victorian times, children adorned themselves with daisy chains on May Day, celebrating the arrival of spring with these delicate floral crowns.
