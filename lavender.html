Lavender

Did you know that Lavandula angustifolia, the 'True Lavender', reigns supreme at our South Island lavender farm? This exceptional variety, widely regarded as the safest and most beloved lavender globally, offers an array of benefits beyond its aromatic allure. While it's renowned for its essential oil, used in aromatherapy, inhalation, and topical applications, there's much more to discover about this remarkable plant. Join us as we unveil ten intriguing and delightful lavender facts:

A Timeless Tradition:
Lavender boasts a rich history spanning over 2,500 years, tracing back to ancient Greece, where it was revered for its medicinal properties and association with biblical events.

A Treasured Ingredient:
In ancient Egypt, lavender played a pivotal role in the mummification process and found its way into various products, from massage oils to medicines.

A Soldier's Ally:
Romans recognized lavender's antiseptic qualities and utilized it on battlefields to dress wounds, showcasing its versatile applications beyond medicinal use.

The Origin of Lavender:
The etymology of lavender can be traced back to the Latin word 'lavare,' meaning 'to wash,' reflecting its historical association with cleanliness and hygiene.

An Ancient Defense:
Lavender's aromatic properties were harnessed during viral epidemics to purify living spaces and ward off negative energies, a practice embraced by ancient civilizations.

The Inspiration Behind a Color:
Lavandula angustifolia, the quintessential lavender species, served as the muse for the delicate hue that bears its name.

Regal Affections:
Queen Victoria's fondness for lavender extended beyond its fragrance; she incorporated it into her daily routines, from furniture polish to tea infusions.

Thriving in Nature's Embrace:
Lavender thrives in arid, sun-kissed environments with well-drained soil, flourishing in our idyllic lavender farm setting.

A Fragrant Tradition:
Dried lavender continues to enchant, adorning linens to impart a fresh aroma, while lavender hydrosol offers a modern twist on this timeless practice.

Symbols of Tranquility:
Lavender flowers symbolize grace, serenity, purity, and calmness, encapsulating the essence of this beloved botanical treasure.
